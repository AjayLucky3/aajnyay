Emergency Resources

If you are facing active threats, financial cybercrime, or need immediate legal aid, use these official verified helplines. Calls from mobile devices will auto-dial.

Cyber Crime Portal
1930

Report online cyber banking fraud, digital transaction loops, identity thefts, or social media blackmail immediately to freeze destination accounts.

Call 1930
Women Helpline
1091

Providing 24-hour immediate assistance, guidance, legal protection references, and emergency evacuation for women facing domestic violence or abuse.

Call 1091
Child Helpline
1098

National 24x7 emergency phone outreach service for children in distress, run by the Ministry of Women and Child Development (WCD).

Call 1098
Senior Citizen (Elderline)
14567

Elderline offers support, rescue links, legal information guidance, and grievance redressal for elderly citizens across India.

Legal Aid Helpline
15100

National Legal Services Authority (NALSA) toll-free number. Offers free legal aid, advice, and court attorney representation to weaker sections.

Call 15100

Free Legal Aid & Services

Under Section 12 of the Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987, eligible citizens (including women, children, SC/ST members, industrial workmen, and persons earning under a specific income cap) have a statutory right to receive free legal aid. This includes attorney representation in courts, drafting petitions, and counseling.

National (NALSA)

Formulates legal services directives, coordinates state bureaus, and governs national lok adalats.

Visit NALSA Portal

State (SALSA)

Each state has a State Legal Services Authority. Coordinates state-level legal clinics and panel lawyers.

Find State Authority

District (DLSA)

Located in every District Court complex. Walk-in centers offer on-the-spot legal advice and counsel appointments.
